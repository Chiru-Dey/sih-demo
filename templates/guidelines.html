Safety Guidelines

Comprehensive disaster preparedness and safety protocols

Earthquake Safety Guidelines

Before an Earthquake

  • Prepare an emergency kit with water, food, flashlight, and first aid supplies
  • Secure heavy furniture and appliances to walls
  • Identify safe spots in each room (under sturdy tables, away from glass)
  • Practice "Drop, Cover, and Hold On" with family members
  • Keep important documents in a waterproof container
  • Learn how to turn off gas, water, and electricity

During an Earthquake

  • Drop: Get down on hands and knees immediately
  • Cover: Take cover under a sturdy table or desk
  • Hold On: Hold onto your shelter and protect your head
  • Stay away from windows, mirrors, and heavy objects
  • If outdoors, move away from buildings, trees, and power lines
  • If driving, pull over safely and stay in the vehicle

After an Earthquake

  • Check yourself and others for injuries
  • Check for hazards like gas leaks, electrical damage, or fires
  • Use stairs, not elevators
  • Stay away from damaged buildings
  • Be prepared for aftershocks
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts for updates

Flood Safety Guidelines

Before a Flood

  • Know your evacuation routes and higher ground locations
  • Keep emergency supplies ready including waterproof containers
  • Install sump pumps and backup power sources
  • Review insurance policies and document belongings
  • Sign up for local emergency alerts
  • Learn how to shut off utilities

During a Flood

  • Move to higher ground immediately
  • Avoid walking or driving through flood waters
  • Stay away from electrical equipment if wet
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts
  • Do not enter flowing water - 6 inches can knock you down
  • If trapped, signal for help from the highest point

After a Flood

  • Wait for authorities to declare the area safe
  • Avoid flood water - it may be contaminated
  • Document damage for insurance claims
  • Clean and disinfect everything that got wet
  • Watch for electrical hazards
  • Boil water until authorities confirm it's safe

Fire Safety Guidelines

Fire Prevention

  • Install smoke detectors and check batteries regularly
  • Keep fire extinguishers in key locations
  • Create and practice a fire escape plan
  • Keep flammable materials away from heat sources
  • Maintain electrical systems and appliances
  • Never leave cooking unattended

During a Fire

  • Get out immediately and call emergency services
  • Feel doors before opening - if hot, use another exit
  • Crawl low under smoke
  • Stop, drop, and roll if clothes catch fire
  • Never use elevators during a fire
  • Meet at your designated meeting point

Smoke Safety

  • Stay low to avoid smoke inhalation
  • Cover nose and mouth with cloth
  • Test doors for heat before opening
  • If trapped, seal door cracks with cloth
  • Signal for help from a window
  • Wait for rescue if escape routes are blocked

Cyclone Safety Guidelines

Before a Cyclone

  • Monitor weather reports and warnings
  • Secure outdoor objects that could become projectiles
  • Stock up on water, food, and emergency supplies
  • Charge all electronic devices
  • Know your evacuation zone and routes
  • Protect windows with boards or tape

During a Cyclone

  • Stay indoors and away from windows
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts
  • Use flashlights, not candles
  • Stay in the strongest part of the building
  • Beware of the eye - winds will return
  • Do not go outside until authorities say it's safe

After a Cyclone

  • Check for injuries and give first aid
  • Inspect your home for damage
  • Avoid downed power lines
  • Use generators outdoors only
  • Photograph damage for insurance
  • Help neighbors who may need assistance

Urban Flood Safety Guidelines

Before an Urban Flood

  • Check for flood alerts and warnings for your area.
  • Clear drains and gutters around your property.
  • Move valuables, electronics, and important documents to higher floors.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with essentials.

During an Urban Flood

  • Do not drive or walk through flooded roads.
  • Avoid contact with floodwater as it may be contaminated.
  • Turn off electricity at the main switch to prevent electrocution.
  • Move to the highest point of your building if trapped.

After an Urban Flood

  • Boil drinking water until authorities declare it safe.
  • Check your home for structural damage before re-entering.
  • Be cautious of snakes, insects, and other animals that may have entered your home.
  • Clean and disinfect everything that was in contact with floodwater.

Tsunami Safety Guidelines

Before a Tsunami

  • Know the tsunami risk for your area and identify evacuation routes to high ground.
  • Prepare an emergency kit for your family.
  • Listen to official warnings and alerts.
  • Practice your evacuation plan with your family.

During a Tsunami

  • If you are in a coastal area and feel a strong earthquake, evacuate to high ground immediately.
  • Do not wait for an official tsunami warning.
  • Move as far inland as possible.
  • A tsunami is a series of waves; do not return to the coast after the first wave.

After a Tsunami

  • Stay away from the coast until authorities declare it safe.
  • Be aware that more waves may be coming.
  • Check for injuries and administer first aid if needed.
  • Listen to the radio for updates and instructions.

Landslides Safety Guidelines

Before a Landslide

  • Determine if your property is in a landslide-prone area.
  • Avoid building near steep slopes or mountain edges.
  • Look for warning signs such as cracks in the ground, tilting trees, or bulging ground.
  • Have an evacuation plan.

During a Landslide

  • Move to a safer location quickly.
  • If you cannot escape, curl into a tight ball and protect your head.
  • Stay away from the path of the landslide.

After a Landslide

  • Stay away from the slide area as there may be a danger of more slides.
  • Check for injured or trapped persons near the slide.
  • Report any broken utility lines to the appropriate authorities.
  • Listen for the latest emergency information.

Drought Safety Guidelines

Before a Drought

  • Conserve water at all times.
  • Install water-saving devices in your home.
  • Use drought-resistant plants in your garden.
  • Have a plan for obtaining water in an emergency.

During a Drought

  • Follow water use restrictions imposed by authorities.
  • Avoid wasting water in any way.
  • Be extremely careful with fire, as dry conditions increase fire risk.

After a Drought

  • Continue to conserve water as it takes time for water sources to recover.
  • Follow instructions from authorities regarding water use.

Cold Wave Safety Guidelines

Before a Cold Wave

  • Insulate your home to keep the heat in.
  • Check your heating systems to ensure they are working properly.
  • Stock up on food, water, and warm clothing.
  • Bring pets indoors.

During a Cold Wave

  • Stay indoors as much as possible.
  • Wear layers of warm clothing.
  • Watch for signs of frostbite and hypothermia.
  • Keep dry. Change wet clothing frequently.

After a Cold Wave

  • Check on elderly or vulnerable neighbors.
  • Be careful of icy roads and walkways to prevent falls.

Thunderstorm Safety Guidelines

Before a Thunderstorm

  • Secure outdoor objects that could be blown away.
  • Unplug sensitive electronics to protect them from power surges.
  • Have a battery-powered radio for updates.
  • Know your emergency plan.

During a Thunderstorm

  • Stay indoors in a safe location.
  • Avoid using corded phones and electrical appliances.
  • Stay away from windows and doors.
  • Do not take a shower or bath.

After a Thunderstorm

  • Wait at least 30 minutes after the last thunder before going outside.
  • Check your property for damage.
  • Stay away from downed power lines.

Cloudburst and Hailstorm Safety Guidelines

Before a Cloudburst/Hailstorm

  • Monitor weather forecasts and warnings.
  • Take shelter in a sturdy building.
  • Bring pets and outdoor furniture inside.

During a Cloudburst/Hailstorm

  • Stay indoors and away from windows and skylights.
  • If you are in a car, pull over to a safe place and cover your head with your arms.
  • Do not go outside to protect property.

After a Cloudburst/Hailstorm

  • Check for damage to your property, including your roof and vehicles.
  • Be careful of fallen debris and downed power lines.

GLOF Safety Guidelines

Before a GLOF

  • Be aware of the risk if you live in an area downstream from a glacial lake.
  • Have an evacuation plan and know the routes to higher ground.
  • Pay attention to warnings from local authorities.

During a GLOF

  • Evacuate immediately to higher ground if you receive a warning or hear a loud, continuous roar.
  • Do not wait for further instructions.
  • Help your neighbors to evacuate.

After a GLOF

  • Do not return to the affected area until authorities declare it safe.
  • The floodwaters may have destabilized the ground.

Heat Wave Safety Guidelines

Before a Heat Wave

  • Install air conditioning or ensure you have access to a cool place.
  • Stay informed about upcoming heat waves.
  • Plan to stay hydrated.

During a Heat Wave

  • Stay in cool, air-conditioned places as much as possible.
  • Drink plenty of water, even if you don't feel thirsty.
  • Avoid strenuous activities during the hottest part of the day.
  • Check on vulnerable family members, friends, and neighbors.

After a Heat Wave

  • Continue to stay hydrated and cool as your body recovers.

Chemical Disaster Safety Guidelines

Before a Chemical Disaster

  • Know the emergency plan for your community and workplace, including warning signals.
  • Have a disaster kit with items to help you shelter-in-place.

During a Chemical Disaster

  • Follow instructions from emergency officials. You may be told to evacuate or shelter-in-place.
  • If sheltering-in-place, go indoors, close all windows and doors, and turn off ventilation systems.

After a Chemical Disaster

  • Do not return to the area until authorities have declared it safe.
  • Follow decontamination procedures if you were exposed.

Nuclear Emergency Safety Guidelines

Before a Nuclear Emergency

  • Know your community's emergency plan and evacuation routes.
  • Have an emergency kit.

During a Nuclear Emergency

  • Follow instructions from emergency officials. You may be told to evacuate or shelter-in-place.
  • If sheltering, go to a basement or the center of a building.
  • Take potassium iodide (KI) only if advised by health officials.

After a Nuclear Emergency

  • Follow decontamination procedures if you have been outside.
  • Listen to authorities for information on when it is safe to leave your shelter.

Biological Emergency Safety Guidelines

Before a Biological Emergency

  • Practice good hygiene, including frequent hand washing.
  • Keep your vaccinations up to date.
  • Have a supply of food, water, and medical supplies.

During a Biological Emergency

  • Follow public health guidelines, which may include social distancing, wearing masks, or quarantine.
  • Seek medical attention if you develop symptoms.
  • Stay informed through official sources.

After a Biological Emergency

  • Continue to follow advice from public health officials.
  • Take care of your mental and physical health.

Forest Fire Safety Guidelines

Before a Forest Fire

  • Create a defensible space around your home by clearing flammable vegetation.
  • Have an evacuation plan and an emergency kit ready.
  • Know multiple ways out of your neighborhood.

During a Forest Fire

  • Evacuate immediately when authorities issue an evacuation order.
  • Follow your evacuation plan.
  • Close all windows and doors before you leave.

After a Forest Fire

  • Do not return home until authorities say it is safe.
  • Check your property for embers or hot spots.
  • Be aware of potential hazards like damaged trees or power lines.

General Emergency Preparedness

Emergency Kit Essentials

  • Water (1 gallon per person per day for 3 days)
  • Non-perishable food (3-day supply)
  • Battery-powered radio and flashlight
  • First aid kit and medications
  • Whistle for signaling help
  • Plastic sheeting and duct tape
  • Cell phone chargers
  • Cash and copies of important documents

Family Emergency Plan

  • Choose two meeting places (near home and outside neighborhood)
  • Designate an out-of-state contact person
  • Plan evacuation routes from home, work, and school
  • Practice emergency drills regularly
  • Keep emergency contacts list updated
  • Ensure all family members know the plan

Communication Plan

  • Program emergency numbers into phones
  • Keep a battery-powered or hand-crank radio
  • Know how to send text messages (often work when calls don't)
  • Register for local emergency alerts
  • Learn about emergency communication methods
  • Have a backup communication plan

First Aid Basics

  • Learn CPR and basic first aid
  • Know how to treat shock
  • Understand how to stop bleeding
  • Learn to recognize signs of stroke and heart attack
  • Keep first aid manual in your emergency kit
  • Take a certified first aid course
